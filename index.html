Before You Begin
  • Always warm up for 5 minutes before touching weight — joint circles, light marching, arm swings
  • Keep water close. Hydrate between rounds, not just after
  • If you feel dizzy, nauseous, or see spots — stop. Sit down. Breathe. Resume only when it passes
  • Sharp pain is a stop sign. Muscle burn is a green light. Know the difference
  • Form first, speed second. A sloppy rep at high speed is a waste and a risk
  • If an exercise doesn't feel right, swap it for the alternative listed or skip it
SECTION ONE

Dynamic Warm-Up

Five minutes. Every session starts here. No exceptions.
Moderate · Every Session

Activation Flow

Get blood moving, joints open, muscles talking to the brain.
01Jumping Jacks
30 seconds
Classic jacks. Arms fully overhead, feet wide. Land soft on the balls of your feet.
CueGet the heart rate up. This is the ignition, not the cruise.
02Leg Swings (Front-to-Back)
10 each leg
Hold a wall or chair for balance. Swing one leg forward and back like a pendulum. Keep the leg straight and the swing controlled.
CueLoosen the hip joint. Range of motion before load.
Watch Form
03Hip Circles
10 each direction
Hands on hips, feet shoulder-width. Draw big slow circles with your hips — clockwise, then counterclockwise.
CueOpen up the hips. You'll need them for every swing and squat today.
04Inchworms
5 reps
Stand tall, fold forward and walk your hands out to a plank. Hold one second. Walk feet to hands. Stand tall. Repeat.
CueFull body primer — hamstrings, core, shoulders, all in one move.
Watch Form
05Bodyweight Squats
10 reps
Feet shoulder-width, toes slightly out. Sit back and down like you're reaching for a low chair. Drive through the heels to stand.
CueGroove the squat pattern before we add load.
SECTION TWO

Kettlebell Circuit

The engine. High-intensity, full-body, fat-burning work. 40 seconds on, 20 seconds rest. Repeat circuit 3–4 times.
High Intensity · KB

Kettlebell Fire

40 seconds work / 20 seconds rest. Rest 90 seconds between rounds. 3–4 rounds total.
01Kettlebell SwingsHigh
40 sec
Hinge at the hips, grip the bell with both hands. Snap the hips forward to swing the bell to chest height. Let it fall back between your legs. The power comes from your hips, not your arms.
CueHips snap. Arms guide. The bell floats because of your glutes, not your shoulders.
Watch Form
02Goblet SquatsHigh
40 sec
Hold the kettlebell by the horns at chest level. Squat deep — elbows inside the knees. Drive up explosively.
CueChest up, elbows inside knees. Drop it low — depth is where the tone happens.
Watch Form
03KB Single-Arm Row (each side)High
40 sec total
Stagger stance, non-working hand on your knee. Row the bell to your hip, squeezing the shoulder blade at the top. 20 seconds per side.
CuePull to the hip, not the armpit. Think about crushing a walnut between your shoulder blades.
Watch Form
04KB DeadliftsHigh
40 sec
Bell on the floor between your feet. Hinge at the hips, flat back, grab the handle. Drive through the heels to stand tall. Squeeze glutes at the top. Lower with control.
CueThis is a hip hinge, not a squat. Push your butt back like closing a car door with your hips.
Watch Form
05KB Around-the-WorldModerate
40 sec
Pass the kettlebell around your waist, switching hands in front and behind. Keep your core tight and hips still. Switch direction halfway.
CueYour core is the anchor. Everything else moves around a stable center.
Watch Form
06KB Sumo High PullsHigh
40 sec
Wide stance, toes out 45 degrees. Squat down, grab the bell. Explode up and pull the bell to chin height, elbows high. Lower and repeat in one fluid motion.
CueThis is a full-body burner. Legs drive, arms finish. Cardio and strength in one move.
Watch Form
SECTION THREE

Dumbbell Sculpt

Targeted toning. 3 sets of 12–15 reps per exercise. 30–45 seconds rest between sets.
High Intensity · DB

Upper Body

3 sets x 12–15 reps. Rest 30–45 seconds between sets. Pick a weight that makes the last 2 reps hard.
01DB Bent-Over RowsHigh
3 x 12 each arm
Hinge forward 45 degrees, flat back. Pull the dumbbell to your hip. Squeeze the back at the top. Lower with control.
CueBack muscles do the work. If you feel it only in your bicep, lighten up and slow down.
Watch Form
02DB Shoulder PressHigh
3 x 12
Standing or seated. Start with dumbbells at shoulder height, palms forward. Press straight overhead until arms are fully extended. Lower to shoulders.
CueCore tight. Don't arch your back — if you have to lean back, the weight is too heavy.
Watch Form
03DB Bicep Curl to PressHigh
3 x 12
Curl both dumbbells to shoulders. Immediately press them overhead. Lower back to shoulders, then lower the curl. That's one rep.
CueTwo moves in one. More muscles working = more calories burning.
Watch Form
04DB Lateral RaisesModerate
3 x 15
Stand with dumbbells at your sides. Raise arms out to the sides until shoulder height, slight bend in elbows. Lower slowly.
CueLight weight, high reps. This is where shoulder definition is built.
Watch Form
05DB Tricep KickbacksModerate
3 x 15 each arm
Hinge forward, elbow pinned at your side at 90 degrees. Extend the arm straight back, squeezing the tricep. Return to 90 degrees.
CueOnly the forearm moves. Elbow stays glued to your ribs.
Watch Form
High Intensity · DB

Lower Body

3 sets x 12–15 reps. These build the glutes, quads, and hamstrings that define your legs.
06DB Sumo SquatsHigh
3 x 15
Wide stance, toes pointed out. Hold one dumbbell with both hands between your legs. Squat deep, keeping your chest upright. Drive up through the heels.
CueInner thighs and glutes. The wider the stance, the more you hit the inner leg.
Watch Form
07DB Romanian DeadliftsHigh
3 x 12
Hold dumbbells in front of your thighs. Hinge at the hips, sliding the weights down your shins. Feel the stretch in your hamstrings. Drive hips forward to stand.
CueThis is where the back of the legs get defined. Slow down the lowering — that's where the magic is.
Watch Form
08DB Reverse LungesHigh
3 x 12 each leg
Hold dumbbells at your sides. Step one foot back, lower until both knees are at 90 degrees. Drive through the front heel to return. Alternate legs.
CueStep back far enough that your front knee doesn't travel past your toes.
Watch Form
09DB Glute BridgesModerate
3 x 15
Lie on your back, feet flat, knees bent. Place one dumbbell on your hips. Drive through the heels and squeeze your glutes at the top. Hold 2 seconds. Lower slowly.
CueSqueeze at the top like you're trying to crack a walnut between your cheeks. That squeeze is everything.
Watch Form
10DB Calf RaisesModerate
3 x 20
Hold dumbbells at your sides. Rise onto the balls of your feet as high as you can. Hold one second. Lower slowly — take 3 seconds down.
CueThe slow lower is where calves actually grow. Don't bounce.
Watch Form
High Intensity · Core

Core Finisher

No rest between exercises. Run straight through all 4 moves. Rest 60 seconds. Repeat 2–3 times.
11Mountain ClimbersHigh
30 sec
Plank position. Drive knees to chest alternately as fast as you can while keeping hips level. Don't let your butt pike up.
CueCardio + core in one. Keep the hips down and the pace up.
Watch Form
12Russian Twists (with DB)High
30 sec
Sit with knees bent, feet off the floor, holding one dumbbell with both hands. Rotate your torso side to side, tapping the weight beside each hip.
CueRotate from the ribcage, not just the arms. Your obliques should be on fire.
Watch Form
13Plank HoldModerate
30–45 sec
Forearms on the ground, body in a straight line from head to heels. Squeeze your glutes, brace your core. Don't let your hips sag or pike.
CueThink about pulling your elbows toward your toes. That activates the deep core.
Watch Form
14Bicycle CrunchesHigh
30 sec
Lie on your back, hands behind your head. Bring opposite elbow to opposite knee, extending the other leg. Alternate in a pedaling motion.
CueSlow and deliberate beats fast and sloppy. Feel each rotation.
Watch Form
SECTION FOUR

Recovery & Stretching

This is where your body actually changes. Training breaks you down. Recovery builds you back up stronger. Never skip this.

Why Recovery Matters

Muscle doesn't grow during the workout — it grows during rest. Skipping recovery means you're only doing the breaking-down part without the building-back-up part. Stretching improves blood flow, reduces soreness, and keeps your range of motion wide so every rep in your next workout is more effective.

Cool-Down · Every Session

Post-Workout Stretches

Hold each stretch 30–45 seconds. Breathe deeply. Never bounce.
01Standing Quad Stretch
30 sec each leg
Stand on one leg (hold a wall if needed). Pull the other foot to your glute. Keep knees together and hips pushed slightly forward.
Why It MattersTight quads pull on the kneecap and limit squat depth. This keeps your legs long and mobile.
Watch Stretch
02Hip Flexor Stretch (Half-Kneeling)
30 sec each side
Kneel on one knee, other foot forward. Squeeze the back glute and shift your hips forward until you feel a deep stretch in the front of the back hip.
Why It MattersSitting all day tightens the hip flexors. Tight hip flexors shut off your glutes. This unlocks them.
Watch Stretch
03Figure-4 Stretch (Piriformis)
30 sec each side
Lie on your back. Cross one ankle over the opposite knee. Pull the bottom thigh toward your chest until you feel a deep stretch in the crossed leg's glute.
Why It MattersReleases the piriformis and deep glute muscles. Prevents lower back tightness and sciatica-like pain.
Watch Stretch
04Chest Doorway Stretch
30 sec
Stand in a doorway, forearms on the frame at shoulder height. Step one foot forward and lean gently through the door until you feel a stretch across your chest.
Why It MattersOpens the chest and front shoulders after all that pressing. Prevents the rounded-shoulder posture.
Watch Stretch
05Cat-Cow Stretch
10 slow reps
On hands and knees. Inhale: drop your belly, lift your chest and tailbone (cow). Exhale: round your spine, tuck chin and pelvis (cat).
Why It MattersMobilizes the entire spine. Decompresses after all the loading. The best back reset there is.
Watch Stretch
06Child's Pose
45–60 sec
Kneel, sit back on your heels, reach your arms forward on the ground. Let your forehead rest down. Breathe into your lower back.
Why It MattersFull-body decompression. Brings your nervous system from fight mode to recovery mode. End every session here.
Watch Stretch

Recovery Day Activities

On rest days, you're not doing nothing — you're recovering actively. Here are the best things you can do:

Foam Rolling (10 min) — Roll quads, hamstrings, glutes, and upper back. Spend 60 seconds on each area. If it hurts, slow down and breathe through it.

Walking (20–30 min) — Easy pace. This flushes metabolic waste from the muscles and improves circulation without adding stress.

Full Stretch Routine (15 min) — Do all 6 stretches above, holding each for 45–60 seconds instead of 30.

Recovery is not weakness. It's strategy. The more you respect it, the harder you can train tomorrow.

SECTION EIGHT

Principles That Carry You

Four ideas to hold in mind when motivation dips.
Principle 01

Intensity over duration.

Thirty focused minutes at high effort will always beat sixty lazy minutes. It's not about how long you train — it's about how hard you work while you're there.

Principle 02

Consistency compounds.

One great workout does nothing. Twenty good workouts change your body. Show up, even when you don't feel like it. Especially when you don't feel like it.

Principle 03

Recovery is strategy, not laziness.

Your muscles don't grow during the workout. They grow while you sleep, stretch, and eat well. Training without recovery is like revving an engine without oil.

Principle 04

The last two reps are the only ones that count.

The first ten reps warm the muscle up. The last two — the hard ones, the ones you want to quit on — those are the ones that actually change your body. Stay for those.

Listen to Your Body

Push hard, but know when to stop. These are the signals that something is wrong.
  • Sharp, stabbing pain in any joint (knees, shoulders, lower back)
  • Dizziness or seeing spots during or after exercise
  • Chest tightness, pressure, or pain
  • Numbness or tingling in hands or feet during exercise
  • Pain that gets worse with each rep instead of better
  • Feeling faint or nauseous — sit down immediately
Muscle burn is normal. Joint pain is not. If something feels wrong, stop the exercise and contact Billy. When in doubt, rest.
